Steelheart By Brandon Sanderson

Our Ratings: 90/100

Community Ratings: Cooming Soon!

Steelheart is an exciting and inventive entry into the young adult dystopian genre. The story is set in a world where people with extraordinary powers, known as Epics, rule with an iron fist, and the main character, David, is on a quest for revenge against one of the most powerful Epics of all: Steelheart. Sanderson's writing is engaging, and his world-building is top-notch as he explores the consequences of superhuman abilities in a gritty and dangerous setting. The story is filled with action, suspense, and a touch of humor, making it a page-turner for readers of all ages. "Steelheart" is a fresh and original take on the superhero genre, and it's a must-read for those who enjoy action-packed adventures and exploring the moral complexities of power and vengeance.

The Fellowship of the Rings By J.R.R. Tolkien

Our Ratings: 95/100

Community Ratings: Cooming Soon!

The Fellowship of the Ring follows the journey of Frodo Baggins and a diverse group of companions as they set out to destroy the One Ring, a malevolent artifact that could lead to the resurrection of the Dark Lord Sauron. Their mission takes them to Mount Doom in Mordor, where the ring must be destroyed. Along the way, they face an array of challenges, including battles with orcs and internal conflicts, all while forging strong bonds of friendship and heroism.

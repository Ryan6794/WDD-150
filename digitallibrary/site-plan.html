Ratings : 90/100 "Steelheart" by Brandon Sanderson is an exciting and inventive entry into the young adult dystopian genre. The story is set in a world where people with extraordinary powers, known as Epics, rule with an iron fist, and the main character, David, is on a quest for revenge against one of the most powerful Epics of all: Steelheart. Sanderson's writing is engaging, and his world-building is top-notch as he explores the consequences of superhuman abilities in a gritty and dangerous setting. The story is filled with action, suspense, and a touch of humor, making it a page-turner for readers of all ages. "Steelheart" is a fresh and original take on the superhero genre, and it's a must-read for those who enjoy action-packed adventures and exploring the moral complexities of power and vengeance. About the Authors

A short description of the author and a list of there works J.R.R. Tolkien, whose full name is John Ronald Reuel Tolkien, was an English writer, poet, philologist, and professor. He is best known for his contributions to the fantasy genre and is considered one of the most influential fantasy authors of the 20th century. Tolkien's works are celebrated for their rich world-building, intricate languages, and epic storytelling. Some of their works: "The Hobbit" (1937) "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy: "The Fellowship of the Ring" (1954) "The Two Towers" (1954) "The Return of the King" (1955) "The Silmarillion" (1977) "The Children of Húrin" (2007) "Unfinished Tales of Númenor and Middle-earth" (1980) "The History of Middle-earth" series "The Book of Lost Tales, Part I" (1983) "The Book of Lost Tales, Part II" (1984) "The Lays of Beleriand" (1985) "The Shaping of Middle-earth" (1986) "The Lost Road and Other Writings" (1987) "The Return of the Shadow" (1988) "The Treason of Isengard" (1989) "The War of the Ring" (1990) "Sauron Defeated" (1992) "Morgoth's Ring" (1993) "The War of the Jewels" (1994) "The Peoples of Middle-earth" (1996)

Brandon Sanderson is a highly acclaimed American author known for his contributions to the fantasy and science fiction genres. He is recognized for his imaginative world-building, intricate magic systems, and compelling characters. Sanderson's notable works include the "Mistborn" series, the "Stormlight Archive" series, and his completion of Robert Jordan's "The Wheel of Time" series. He is also known for his meticulous and prolific writing, engaging with his readers through social media, and his contributions to the genre as both an author and educator, as he teaches creative writing at Brigham Young University. Sanderson has gained a devoted following of readers who appreciate his unique storytelling style and his dedication to expanding the boundaries of speculative fiction Some of their works: Mistborn Trilogy: "Mistborn: The Final Empire" "Mistborn: The Well of Ascension" "Mistborn: The Hero of Ages" Stormlight Archive Series: "The Way of Kings" "Words of Radiance" "Oathbringer" "Rhythm of War" (as of my last knowledge update in January 2022, this book was scheduled for release) The Reckoners Series: "Steelheart" "Firefight" "Calamity" Elantris (standalone novel) Warbreaker (standalone novel) White Sand (a graphic novel series) Alcatraz vs. the Evil Librarians Series (a humorous fantasy series): "Alcatraz vs. the Evil Librarians" "The Scrivener's Bones" "The Knights of Crystallia" "The Shattered Lens" "The Dark Talent" Legion Series: "Legion" "Legion: Skin Deep" "Legion: Lies of the Beholder" The Rithmatist (young adult fantasy) Skyward Series: "Skyward" "Starsight" The Wheel of Time (completed Robert Jordan's epic fantasy series with the final three books): "The Gathering Storm" "Towers of Midnight" "A Memory of Light"
